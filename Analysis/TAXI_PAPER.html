DATA COLLECTION AND METHODOLOGY

The data for the analysis of Croatian taxi market disruption due to Uber is collected from the online edition of newspaper Veernji list. First, we collected all articles (167 600) published in the period November, 2015 to September, 2018 from Vecernji list with web-scraping methods and second, retrieved all articles that contain at least one occurence of the word Uber in the article. This procedure resulted in 370 Uber related articles which were further filtered by hand to exclude unrelated, promotional or otherwise insignificant articles. After the filtering procedure, 165 articles containing 35888 words strictly related to Uber were left for the analysis as our empirical sample. Metadata of our empirical text corpus contains information on the title, author, publishing date, keywords,number of comments and views. We extended our sample with the one more variable that represents one of the five topics that we ascribe to every article. To prepare the text for the analysis, the corpus was tokenized to one (unigram) and two (bigram) word units, stop words and numbers were removed and lemmatisation and stemming was applied.

Methodology

We analyze Uber text corpus with standard descriptive statistics like word count, word occurrences through time, term frequency, word correlations and sentiment analysis. The term frequency (tf) measures how often a word occurs in a document and inverse term frequency (itf) extends this concept by decreasing the weight for commonly used words and increasing the weight for words that are not used often a collection of documents. We combine these two concepts to calculate a terms TF-IDF statistic by the multiplication of the two quantities (tf and idf) and achieve a measure of the frequency of a term adjusted for how rarely it is used. Word correlations count common pairs of words co-appearing within the same newspaper article and we use this measure to investigate terms correlated with most important words.

The sentiment analysis is made on the basis of the Croatian Sentiment Lexicon (CroSentiLex) (http://takelab.fer.hr/sentilex) which contains 37000 words ranked by positivity and negativity according to the algorithmically assigned scores. The analyzed text corpus was merged with CroSentiLex dictionary to attribute each word with the positivity and negativity score. The difference in scores between positive and negative words was calculated and the nett scores per word were multiplied by the number of respective word occurrences in each time period (quarter) to attain the aggregate measure of sentiment. Higher sentiment score is therefore related to more positive sentiment and vice versa.
